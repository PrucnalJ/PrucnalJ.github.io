CMU Lavner Education Instructor

Camp Instructor Intern – Lavner Education at Carnegie Mellon University

As a Camp Instructor Intern at Lavner Education's summer camp hosted at Carnegie Mellon University, I played a pivotal role in teaching children about the exciting world of computer science. My primary responsibilities included sharing my enthusiasm for the field and creating an engaging learning environment that made complex concepts accessible and enjoyable for young learners.

Key Responsibilities:

Coding Instruction:

Delivered hands-on lessons in programming languages such as Python, Java, GML, and Lua, helping campers develop their coding skills through interactive projects.

Minecraft and 3D Innovation:

Guided students in exploring Minecraft modding and 3D design, fostering creativity and problem-solving in a virtual environment.

App Development:

Introduced campers to the basics of app development using MIT App Inventor, allowing them to conceptualize and build their own simple applications.

Artificial Intelligence:

Provided foundational knowledge in artificial intelligence, demonstrating its applications and potential in a way that resonated with the campers. We used applications such as Scratch, RoboCode, GameMaker, and ChatBots.

Roblox Studio Game Design:

Facilitated learning in Roblox Studio, enabling campers to design and develop their own games, enhancing their understanding of game mechanics and design principles.

Throughout the internship, I focused on blending educational content with fun activities, ensuring that each child not only learned valuable technical skills but also enjoyed the process. My role required patience, creativity, and effective communication to inspire and engage young minds in the ever-evolving world of technology.
